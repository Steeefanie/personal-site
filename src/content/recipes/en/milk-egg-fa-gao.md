## Ingredients

- Plain or medium-protein flour, 200 g
- Milk, 100 g for the softer version or 80 g for the springier version
- Eggs, 2
- Caster sugar, 30 g
- Dried yeast, 2.5 g
- Milk powder, 30 g
- Butter, 10 g

Only the milk quantity changes. The 100 g version has a wetter batter and a softer, airier crumb; the 80 g version is thicker and produces more spring and chew.

## Shared method

1. Crack the eggs into a large bowl. Add the sugar, milk, and milk powder and stir until even, dissolving as much of the sugar and powder as possible.
2. Add the dried yeast and mix again.
3. Add the flour and stir thoroughly until no dry patches or large lumps remain, forming a smooth, fairly thick batter.
4. Cover the bowl and leave it somewhere warm for the first proof, until roughly doubled in volume.
5. Melt the butter and brush it evenly over the base and sides of a heatproof steaming bowl.
6. Stir the proved batter thoroughly with chopsticks to release large bubbles and make the structure even again.
7. Pour the batter into the buttered bowl and level the surface.
8. Set the bowl in the steamer without turning on the heat. Rest for 15 minutes for the second proof.
9. Steam for 30 minutes. Turn off the heat, wait 5 minutes, then uncover and remove the cake.

## Results

- **Softer version:** a fine, moist crumb with pronounced milk, egg, and butter aromas; softer and more open overall.
- **Springier version:** a similar flavour with more bounce and chew rather than an especially fluffy texture.

The butter makes the base and edges more aromatic. Slight browning or natural cracks in places are normal.
