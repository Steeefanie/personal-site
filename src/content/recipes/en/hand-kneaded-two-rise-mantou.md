## Ingredients

- 300 g plain or medium-protein flour;
- 10 g sugar;
- 140 g water, reserving about 10 g to adjust the dough;
- 2 g dried yeast;
- 6–8 g room-temperature water for dissolving the yeast.

## Kneading and first proof

1. Put the flour, sugar, and most of the water in a large bowl, leaving the yeast aside and reserving about 10 g water.
2. Stir with chopsticks until no dry flour remains on the bottom, then bring the flakes together into a dough.
3. Knead for 1½–2 minutes and rest for 10–15 minutes. Repeat for three cycles, until the dough, bowl, and hands are all clean.
4. Seal the dough and refrigerate it for 1–2 hours to relax.
5. Mix the yeast with 6–8 g room-temperature water. Knead it into the dough for 8–10 minutes until evenly distributed, keeping the dough at or below 27°C.
6. Prove at about 30°C for 1–1½ hours, until doubled. A floured finger pressed into the dough should leave a hole that neither springs back quickly nor collapses.
7. Divide into 6 pieces of about 75 g each.
8. Knead each piece for 100–150 strokes, then round it until smooth. Allow about 15–20 minutes to shape all six buns.

## Second proof and steaming

1. Keep the shaped buns moist and prove them at room temperature, in a switched-off oven, or in a steamer set over warm water. At about 30°C, allow 30–60 minutes; a cold proof at about 5°C takes 6–15 hours.
2. They are ready when visibly larger and noticeably lighter. Leave headroom under the lid equal to roughly 1.25–2 times the bun height.
3. Start over cold water if the buns are slightly under-proved; use already-hot water when they are fully proved.
4. Once the steamer is producing full steam, cook for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and wait 5 minutes before lifting the lid.

## Storage

Cool completely before sealing. Keep at room temperature for 2–3 days, or freeze once cool if they will not be eaten within 2 days.
